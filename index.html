The Task

Imagine that you are a volunteer for NYC, and you have been tasked with designing a square info-card that summarizes some information on SNAP, a food benefits program. The information you are given is kind of in a raw form—just a bunch of notes, really:

Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)
SNAP (formerly known as food stamps) helps low-income individuals and families afford nutritious food. To apply online, use ACCESS HRA, where you can create an account and check if you are eligible for benefits. You can also receive a paper application by visiting a SNAP center or calling the Infoline at 718-557-1399. The application process may take up to a month. If you are currently waiting on your application, you can check the status of your case at a SNAP center. If you already get SNAP, you can check your EBT card balance and your time limit online at ACCESS HRA.

In this activity, we would like you to take 5 minutes to spruce this up—organize and format this information in a way that is easy to use, read, and navigate by using space, text size, color, bold, etc. You can change the wording, but make sure to include all the original information!
